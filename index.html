The problem

Being misread is structural, not personal

"You can't read your own label from inside the jar."

The gap between who you are and how you're perceived isn't a confidence problem. It isn't a likability problem. It's a signal problem. Something in the way you communicate, present, or position yourself is being interpreted differently from how you intend it — and you can't see it because you're too close.

This happens to highly capable people constantly. They get filed into the wrong category. Passed over for things they're clearly right for. Respected but not selected. Liked but not taken seriously. Known but not called.

The signals causing this are specific, identifiable, and fixable. That's what this diagnostic is for.

  • You're consistently underestimated by people who don't know you yet
  • You feel like you have to prove yourself in rooms you've already earned
  • People assume things about you that are frustratingly inaccurate
  • You leave conversations feeling like something got lost in translation
  • The version of you that others describe doesn't quite match who you are
